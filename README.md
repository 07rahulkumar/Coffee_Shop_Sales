# Coffee Shop Sales

☕ Coffee Shop Sales Analysis (Excel Project)

 # Project Overview

This project analyzes retail sales data of a Coffee Shop to generate actionable insights that improve sales performance, understand customer behavior, and support data-driven decision making.

The complete analysis and dashboard were built using Microsoft Excel with Pivot Tables, Charts, and Slicers.

 ## Project Summary
The dashboard provides an overview of:

- Total Sales: $698,812.33
- Total Footfall: 149,116 customers
- Average Bill per Person: $4.69
- Average Orders per Person: 1.44

These metrics help understand overall business performance and customer purchasing behavior.

### Business Questions Answered 

1️. Sales by Day & Hour
- Peak sales occur between 8 AM – 10 AM.
- Morning hours contribute the highest order volume.
- Friday has the highest weekday orders (~21,701).
- Saturday shows slightly lower performance (~20,510).
- Insight: Staffing and inventory planning should focus on morning rush hours.

2️. Monthly Sales Analysis
- Monthly performance can be filtered using slicers (January–June).
- Helps identify revenue trends and seasonal variations.

3️. Sales by Store Location
- Store Location	Footfall	Sales
- Astoria	50,599	$232,243.91
- Hell's Kitchen	50,735	$236,511.17
- Lower Manhattan	47,782	$230,057.25

Insight: Hell’s Kitchen generates the highest revenue, but all stores show relatively balanced performance.

4️. Product Category Distribution
- Coffee contributes 39% of total sales (highest).
- Bakery contributes 12%.
- Other categories contribute smaller portions.
- Insight: Coffee is the primary revenue driver.

5️. Top 5 Products by Sales
- Barista Espresso – $91,406.20
- Brewed Chai Tea – $77,081.95
- Hot Chocolate – $72,416.00
- Gourmet Brewed Coffee – $70,034.60
- Brewed Black Tea – $47,932.00

Insight: Barista Espresso is the best-selling product.

6️. Order Size Distribution
- Regular – 31%,
- Large – 30%,
- Not Defined – 30%,
- Small – 9%
Most customers prefer Regular and Large sizes.
